## AI Tooling

- [Cursor Blog](https://cursor.com/blog/cursor-3) Cursor 3 launched April 2 with an Agents Window that runs multiple agents in parallel across local, cloud, worktree, and remote SSH environments. New Design Mode lets users annotate browser UI elements directly to guide agents; multi-repo workspaces and seamless cloud-to-local handoff are also included.

- [GitHub Blog](https://github.blog/news-insights/company-news/updates-to-github-copilot-interaction-data-usage-policy/) Starting April 24, GitHub will use interaction data from Copilot Free, Pro, and Pro+ users to train AI models unless users opt out. Copilot Business and Enterprise subscribers are not affected; opt-out is available in Copilot settings under profile.

- [Releasebot / OpenClaw](https://releasebot.io/updates/openclaw) OpenClaw v2026.4.1 released: adds a chat-native /tasks board for background task tracking, a bundled SearXNG provider plugin for web search, and Amazon Bedrock Guardrails support in the bundled provider.

## Open Source

- [NousResearch GitHub](https://github.com/NousResearch/hermes-agent) NousResearch Hermes Agent reached approximately 27k GitHub stars and is trending at nearly 10k stars this week. Designed as a general-purpose agent framework that adapts over time; Apache 2.0 licensed.

- [Google Research GitHub](https://github.com/google-research/timesfm) Google Research's TimesFM time-series foundation model entered the weekly trending chart with over 4,700 stars this week — the first non-agent-tooling repo to break into the top 8 in several days.

## Security

- [Crain's Grand Rapids Business](https://www.crainsgrandrapids.com/manufacturing-logistics/cgr-stryker-cyber-attack-20260403/) At least two employee lawsuits have been filed against Stryker following the pro-Iran cyberattack that wiped more than 200,000 devices across 79 countries in March. Stryker says systems are fully restored; litigation alleges the company failed to protect employee personal data.

- [The Hacker News / DEV Community](https://dev.to/mrcomputerscience/breaking-cybersecurity-news-for-20260404-pithy-cyborg-threats-breaches-intel-bok) Akira ransomware claimed a breach of Andorra's Pyrénées Group, alleging exfiltration of 263 GB of data including names, email addresses, and payment information. Pyrénées Group confirmed the incident did not result in a ransom payment.

## Geopolitics

- [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/4/6/iran-war-live-tehran-rejects-trumps-tuesday-deadline-on-strait-of-hormuz) Trump's 9 PM EDT April 6 Hormuz deadline arrived with Iran rejecting the ultimatum outright. Iranian officials called the threat an "incitement to war crimes." An Iranian missile struck a residential building in Haifa, killing at least two people.
