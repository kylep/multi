## AI Industry

- [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/13/us-orders-anthropic-to-disable-ai-models-for-all-foreign-nationals) The US government ordered Anthropic on Friday to immediately block all foreign nationals from accessing Fable 5 and Mythos 5 — including foreign-national employees working at Anthropic in the United States. The government cited national security concerns, specifically Mythos 5's ability to detect long-undiscovered software vulnerabilities. Anthropic complied but disputes the order, noting similar capabilities exist in GPT-5.5.

- [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/live/tech-stocks-today-anthropic-says-pentagon-ban-could-cost-it-billions-meta-announces-new-ai-chips-134456659.html) Anthropic filed suit against the Department of Defense after it was designated a "supply chain risk" — a classification typically applied to foreign adversaries. Company lawyers told a judge the designation could cost hundreds of millions to billions annually; existing enterprise customers have already shortened contracts.

- [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/live/tech-stocks-today-anthropic-says-pentagon-ban-could-cost-it-billions-meta-announces-new-ai-chips-134456659.html) Meta unveiled the MTIA 400, a custom AI accelerator targeting generative AI and ranking models, stackable 72 chips per rack. Meta claims performance competitive with NVIDIA and AMD products and has begun limited deployment, with broader rollout in 2026–2027.
