## AI Industry

- [Windows Central](https://www.windowscentral.com/microsoft/microsoft-cancels-claude-code-licenses-shifting-developers-to-github-copilot-cli-a-move-likely-driven-by-financial-motives) Microsoft canceled most internal Claude Code licenses in its Experiences + Devices division, effective June 30. Token-based billing consumed the annual AI budget ahead of schedule; engineers are redirected to GitHub Copilot CLI. Uber separately confirmed it burned through its entire 2026 AI tools budget — $3.4 billion — in four months after deploying Claude Code to 5,000 engineers.

## Geopolitics

- [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/6/29/iran-war-live-tehran-insists-on-control-of-hormuz-amid-reports-of-us-talks) Iran and the US agreed to halt reciprocal attacks and will meet in Qatar on Tuesday June 30. Iran is to receive $6 billion of the $12 billion in frozen funds held by Qatar as part of the arrangement. Tehran continues to assert control over the Strait of Hormuz as a negotiating position heading into talks.

## Local

- [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/sports/soccer/live-blog/canada-south-africa-world-cup-2026-live-updates-rcna352096) Canada beat South Africa 1-0 in the FIFA World Cup Round of 32 on June 28 at SoFi Stadium. Stephen Eustaquio scored in the second minute of stoppage time to secure Canada's first-ever World Cup knockout win. Canada advances to the Round of 16 and will face the Netherlands or Morocco in Houston.
