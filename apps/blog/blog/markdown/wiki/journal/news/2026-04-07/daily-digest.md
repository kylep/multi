## AI Industry

- [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-06/openai-anthropic-google-unite-to-combat-model-copying-in-china) OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google are sharing adversarial distillation data through the Frontier Model Forum to identify Chinese AI companies — including DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax — that are systematically extracting outputs from US frontier models to improve their own. Anthropic documented 16 million suspect exchanges. The three companies have asked the Trump administration for clearer antitrust guidance to allow broader data-sharing.

## AI Tooling

- [How2Shout](https://www.how2shout.com/news/microsoft-copilot-critique-council-claude-gpt-multi-model-research.html) Microsoft launched Copilot Researcher with two new multi-model modes: Critique (one model drafts, the other reviews before output reaches the user) and Council (both Claude and GPT run in parallel and produce independent reports on the same query). Critique scored 57.4 on the standard deep research benchmark, a 13.8% improvement over single-model approaches.

## Security

- [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/04/285-million-drift-hack-traced-to-six.html) North Korean hackers spent six months posing as a quantitative trading firm to build relationships with Drift Protocol contributors, then used that access to socially engineer multisig signers into pre-authorizing dormant transactions via Solana's durable nonces feature. Combined with fake oracle collateral manipulation, they drained $285 million in roughly 12 minutes on April 1 — the largest DeFi hack of 2026 and the 18th attributed DPRK crypto theft this year.

## Geopolitics

- [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/4/7/iran-war-live-trump-warns-of-devastating-attacks-as-deal-deadline-nears) Trump set a firm 8 PM ET deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz or face strikes on power plants and bridges. The IDF conducted overnight strikes on Tehran; a ceasefire proposal from Egypt, Pakistan, and Turkey was rejected by Iran, which is demanding a permanent end to the conflict and reparations. Oil traded above $114/barrel as the deadline approached.

## Local
