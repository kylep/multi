## AI Industry

- [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/24/google-to-invest-up-to-40-billion-in-anthropic-as-search-giant-spreads-its-ai-bets.html) Google is investing up to $40B in Anthropic — $10B now at a $350B valuation with another $30B contingent on performance targets. Google Cloud will provide 5 gigawatts of compute capacity to Anthropic over five years as part of the deal.

- [Microsoft Blog](https://blogs.microsoft.com/blog/2026/04/27/the-next-phase-of-the-microsoft-openai-partnership/) Microsoft and OpenAI restructured their partnership: OpenAI can now serve all its products across any cloud provider (including AWS and Google Cloud), Microsoft's IP license to OpenAI's technology becomes non-exclusive through 2032, and the AGI clause is removed. Microsoft stops paying a revenue share to OpenAI; OpenAI's payments to Microsoft are capped and continue through 2030.

## AI Tooling

- [The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/tech/tech-news/2026/04/28/accenture-to-roll-out-copilot-to-all-743000-employees-in-boost-for-microsoft) Accenture will deploy Microsoft 365 Copilot to all 743,000 of its employees — the largest single enterprise Copilot rollout to date. Internal surveys of 200,000 users found 97% said Copilot helped complete routine tasks up to 15 times faster.

## Security

- [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/04/microsoft-patches-entra-id-role-flaw.html) Microsoft patched a flaw in Entra ID's Agent ID Administrator role — a built-in role for managing AI agent identities — that allowed users to take over arbitrary service principals beyond agent identities, enabling full tenant takeover if the targeted principal held elevated permissions. Silverfort reported the flaw March 1; Microsoft patched it April 9.
