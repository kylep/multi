## AI Industry

- [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/07/anthropic-claude-mythos-ai-hackers-cyberattacks.html) Anthropic is limiting the rollout of Claude Mythos Preview to a select group of companies via a program called Project Glasswing, citing concerns that the model's ability to find and exploit software vulnerabilities could be weaponized if released broadly.

- [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-07/google-adds-mental-health-tools-to-gemini-chatbot-after-lawsuit) Google added crisis-intervention features to Gemini — including a support hotline interface and a "help is available" module — following a wrongful death lawsuit related to a South Florida teenager's suicide. Google also pledged $30M to mental health hotlines.

## AI Tooling

- [GitHub Changelog](https://havoptic.com/tools/github-copilot) GitHub Copilot released v1.0.18 on April 4 with a Critic agent that automatically reviews plans and complex implementations, and v1.0.19 on April 6 with `/mcp enable` and `/mcp disable` now persisting across sessions.

## Security

- [CISA](https://www.cisa.gov/known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog) CISA added CVE-2026-33017, an actively exploited remote code execution flaw in Langflow AI pipeline infrastructure, to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog. Federal agencies must apply patches immediately.

## Geopolitics

- [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/live-updates/iran-war-trump-deadline-power-plants-human-chains-israel-train-strikes/) The US and Iran agreed to a two-week ceasefire ahead of Trump's April 8 Hormuz deadline. Iran will open the Strait; negotiations mediated by Pakistan begin in Islamabad. Oil fell 13% and S&P 500 futures rose more than 2% on the news.
