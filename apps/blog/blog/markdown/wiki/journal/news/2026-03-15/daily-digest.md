## Policy & Regulation

**US Commerce Department withdraws planned AI chip export rule**
The Commerce Department on March 14 quietly withdrew a draft rule that would have required permits for global AI chip exports, according to Reuters and Bloomberg. A US official said the rule "was always a draft," but former officials attributed the withdrawal to unresolved disagreements within the Trump administration over how to balance AI export competitiveness with national security. [Reuters via Yahoo Finance](https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/us-commerce-department-withdraws-planned-214104113.html) | [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-14/us-withdraws-draft-rule-that-called-for-global-ai-chip-permits)

**EU Council adopts position to streamline AI Act, bans nudification tools**
The European Council formally agreed on March 13 to a package of AI Act amendments, including an explicit prohibition on AI tools that generate non-consensual sexual and intimate imagery. The Council's position also eases compliance requirements for AI embedded in sector-regulated products such as medical devices. The amendments now move to trilogue negotiations with the European Parliament. [Council of the EU press release](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/03/13/council-agrees-position-to-streamline-rules-on-artificial-intelligence/) | [The Record](https://therecord.media/european-council-includes-nudification-ban-ai-act)

## Industry & Companies

**Meta reportedly weighing layoffs of up to 20% of workforce**
Reuters reported on March 14, citing three sources, that Meta is considering workforce reductions affecting as many as 20% of employees—approximately 16,000 people—as the company seeks to offset surging AI infrastructure costs. Meta disputed the report, calling it "speculative reporting about theoretical approaches." No timeline has been set. [Reuters via CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/14/meta-planning-sweeping-layoffs-as-ai-costs-mount-reuters.html) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/14/meta-reportedly-considering-layoffs-that-could-affect-20-of-the-company/)

**xAI scraps AI coding tool, poaches senior engineers from Cursor**
Elon Musk acknowledged that xAI "was not built right the first time" and is rebuilding its AI coding division from scratch after internal efforts failed to compete with Claude Code and OpenAI Codex. TechCrunch reported on March 13 that 9 of xAI's original 11 co-founders have now departed, and xAI has hired Andrew Milich and Jason Ginsberg—senior product and engineering leaders from Cursor—to lead the rebuild. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/13/not-built-right-the-first-time-musks-xai-is-starting-over-again-again/) | [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/13/elon-musk-xai-co-founders-spacex-ipo.html)

**Meta delays "Avocado" model to at least May 2026, considers Gemini licensing**
Meta has postponed the release of its next-generation AI model, internally codenamed "Avocado," after internal benchmarks showed it underperforming rival models, according to reporting aggregated by multiple outlets on March 14. The company is reportedly in preliminary discussions with Google about temporarily licensing Gemini technology as a stopgap. Meta has not publicly commented on the model's status. [TopAIProduct](https://topaiproduct.com/2026/03/14/only-2-of-11-co-founders-left-xai-hires-cursors-top-engineers-to-rebuild-its-coding-tool-from-scratch/)

## Products & Tools

**Anthropic launches Claude Partner Network with $100M commitment**
Anthropic announced the Claude Partner Network on March 12, committing an initial $100 million to build an enterprise consulting ecosystem around Claude. Anchor partners include Accenture (which plans to train 30,000 professionals on Claude), Deloitte (which has opened Claude access to roughly 350,000 associates), Cognizant, and Infosys. The network includes a new Claude Certified Architect credential and a Code Modernization starter kit. [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-partner-network) | [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/anthropic-commits-100m-to-claude-partner-network)

**Google Maps launches Gemini-powered "Ask Maps" and Immersive Navigation**
Google announced on March 12 that Google Maps is gaining a conversational AI feature called Ask Maps, powered by Gemini, which allows natural-language queries such as "Where can I charge my phone without waiting in a long coffee line?" The update also includes a redesigned Immersive Navigation experience with 3D visuals and real-time road details. Ask Maps is rolling out now in the US and India on Android and iOS. [Google Blog](https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/maps/ask-maps-immersive-navigation/) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/12/google-maps-is-getting-an-ai-ask-maps-feature-and-upgraded-immersive-navigation/)

## Hardware & Infrastructure

**NVIDIA GTC 2026 preview: $1 trillion AI infrastructure buildout**
Ahead of NVIDIA's GPU Technology Conference opening March 16 in San Jose, SiliconAngle published an analysis on March 14 framing the AI hardware race as a potential $1 trillion infrastructure buildout. The piece highlights HBM memory as a key supply bottleneck (potentially 30% of hyperscaler capex by 2026) and ASML EUV lithography machines as the ultimate production ceiling. Jensen Huang is expected to unveil details on Rubin-generation GPUs, the Vera CPU, and NVIDIA's agentic AI platform at the event. [SiliconAngle](https://siliconangle.com/2026/03/14/gtc-preview-inside-ai-factory-1t-infrastructure-war-hood-ai-economy/)

## Research & Labor Market

**Stanford economists present hard data on AI's impact on entry-level hiring**
Economists presenting at Stanford's SIEPR economic summit reported that entry-level software developer hiring has declined 20% and call center positions are down 15%, attributing the drop to enterprise AI adoption. Researchers warned of growing inequality as AI displaces lower-wage and entry-level roles disproportionately. (Single-source reporting as of March 14; primary paper not yet published.)
